51 """
52 BSplineInterpolation{T,N,TCoefs,IT,Axs}
53
54 An interpolant-type for b-spline interpolation on a uniform grid with integer nodes.
55 `T` indicates the element type for operations like `collect(itp)`, and may also agree with
56 the values obtained from `itp(x, y, ...)` at least for certain types of `x` and `y`.
57 `N` is the dimensionality of the interpolant. The remaining type-parameters
58 describe the types of fields:
59 - the `coefs` field holds the interpolation coefficients. Depending on prefiltering,
60 these may or may not be the same as the supplied array of interpolant values.
61 - `parentaxes` holds the axes of the parent. Depending on prefiltering this may be
62 "narrower" than the axes of `coefs`.
63 - `it` holds the interpolation type, e.g., `BSpline(Linear())` or `(BSpline(Quadratic(OnCell()),BSpline(Linear()))`.
64
65 `BSplineInterpolation` objects are typically created with [`interpolate`](@ref).
66 However, for customized control you may also construct them with
67
68 BSplineInterpolation(TWeights, coefs, it, axs)
69
70 where `T` gets computed from the product of `TWeights` and `eltype(coefs)`.
71 (This is equivalent to indicating that you'll be evaluating at locations `itp(x::TWeights, y::TWeights, ...)`.)
72 """

194 """
195 itp = interpolate(A, interpmode, gridstyle, λ, k)
196
197 Interpolate an array `A` in the mode determined by `interpmode` and `gridstyle`
198 with regularization following [1], of order `k` and constant `λ`.
199 `interpmode` may be one of
200
201 - `BSpline(NoInterp())`
202 - `BSpline(Linear())`
203 - `BSpline(Quadratic(BC()))` (see [`BoundaryCondition`](@ref))
204 - `BSpline(Cubic(BC()))`
205
206 It may also be a tuple of such values, if you want to use different interpolation schemes along each axis.
207
208 `gridstyle` should be one of `OnGrid()` or `OnCell()`.
209
210 `k` corresponds to the derivative to penalize. In the limit λ->∞, the interpolation function is
211 a polynomial of order `k-1`. A value of 2 is the most common.
212
213 `λ` is non-negative. If its value is zero, it falls back to non-regularized interpolation.
214
215 [1] https://projecteuclid.org/euclid.ss/1038425655.
216 """
